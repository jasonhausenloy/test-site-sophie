About

I'm Sophie, a Stanford student (Class of 2030) currently on a gap year to focus on AI safety research. I'm majoring in Symbolic Systems, drawn to the intersection of cognitive science, philosophy, and computation.

My work centers on ensuring advanced AI systems remain beneficial and under meaningful human control. I research biosecurity risks from AI, policy interventions for emerging threats, and institutional mechanisms for responsible AI development.

I'm the youngest person admitted to the ERA Fellowship (<1% acceptance rate), where I'll be conducting research at the Cambridge Biosecurity Hub on tiered access controls for advanced AI/biology capabilities.

Beyond research, I love reading, traveling, meeting new people, and diving into philosophy, psychology, and symbolic logic. I'm fluent in Spanish and always open to interesting conversations.
